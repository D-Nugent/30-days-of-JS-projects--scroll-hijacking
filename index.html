These are the facts

According to sustainable facts about 80% of the ítems that Americans throw away can be recycled. However, currently, America’s recycling rate is at only 28%.

Environmental sustainability facts show that enough plastic is thrown away each year to circle the earth four times.

An average American family can use up to 260 gallons of water at home per day.

According to sustainability facts, energy is the number one contributor to climate change, accounting for around 60% of total global greenhouse gas emissions.

The average American uses 650 pounds of paper each year. If all this paper was recycled, we could save up to 100 million tons of wood every year.

Americans use over 2.5 million plastic bottles every thirty minutes. Most of these bottles are simply thrown away even though they can be recycled.

There is more carbon dioxide in the atmosphere today than at any point in the last 800,000 years.

We need to reduce carbon dioxide emissions by humans to 45% from 2010 levels by 2030 and reach net-zero by 2050 to avoid a climate change catastrophe.

500,000 trees are cut down to produce Sunday newspapers each week. If you were to recycle your copy of the Sunday edition of the New York Times, you alone could save 75,000 trees. And if we all recycled all our newspapers, we could save about 250,000,000 trees each year!

It takes a 15-year-old tree to produce 700 grocery bags.

Since 1870, global sea levels have risen by about 8 inches.

Sustainability facts show that cycling 10 kilometers each way to work can save you about $1,700 in transport costs but most importantly, you will save the world 1,500 kilograms of greenhouse gas emissions every year.

Recycling one aluminum can saves enough energy to listen to a full album on your iPod. Recycling 100 cans could light your bedroom for two whole weeks.

Sustainability fun facts have revealed that if just one person goes vegan, they can save approximately 219,000 gallons of water a year.

People who live in places with high levels of air pollutants have a 20% higher risk of death from lung cancer than people who live in less-polluted areas

Australians could save about 800,000 megalitres of water, which is more than the whole of Sydney Harbour just by using more water-efficient appliances and products.

When you leave your computer monitors on overnight they emit 9 million tons of carbon dioxide every year.

Approximately 40% of the lakes in America are too polluted for fishing, aquatic life, or swimming.

If you replace one old-style incandescent globe with a compact fluorescent light you will save around $40 in electricity costs. It will also last six times longer.

While children make up 10% of the world’s population, over 40% of the global burden of disease falls on them. More than 3 million children under age five die annually from environmental factors
